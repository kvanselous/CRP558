Process_Step:
Process_Description:
-The original data arrives as paper, or possibly Mylar or digital maps of the drainage districts, and ditch and tile lines. Often, there will be several maps for a given area, as districts are updated. The maps are sorted, and compared to remove any unneeded maps.

-Two blank coverages are created. One for the district and sub district outlines, and the other for the tiles and ditches. A system of tics is created with tics at either township or section corners. Sometimes during digitizing, additional tics must be created for maps whose area does not include 4 tics.

-The maps are then digitized in ADS, a subprogram of PC Arc Info.

-After digitizing, the coverages are overlaid, inspected, and corrected as needed in PC Arc Edit.

-The coverages are imported into ArcView, and converted to shape files. Then they are rechecked, and corrected as needed. Corrections and additions are at this point made in ArcView.

-Attribute fields are added, and filled.

-Shapefiles are given a final spot check. This is done by someone other then the person originally assigned to do the ArcView work.
