Overview_Description:
Entity_and_Attribute_Overview:
AIRPORTS_I: Unique identifier for each landing facility.

FACILITY_T: Landing facility type. Possible types are:

AIRPORT

BALLOONPORT

SEAPLANE BASE

GLIDERPORT

HELIPORT

STOLPORT

ULTRALIGHT

LOC_IDENT: Location Identifier. A unique 3-4 character alphanumeric identifier assigned to the landing facility (EX. 'ORD' for Chicago O'Hare.)

INFO_EFF_D: Information effective date. (MM/DD/YY)

FAA_REGION: FAA REGION CODE

CODE REGION NAME

AAL ALASKA

ACE CENTRAL

AEA EASTERN

AGL GREAT LAKES

AIN INTERNATIONAL

ANE NEW ENGLAND

ANM NORTHWEST MOUNTAIN

ASO SOUTHERN

ASW SOUTHWEST

AWP WESTERN-PACIFIC

FAA_DISTRI: FAA District or field office code. (EX. CHI for Chicago)

STATE: Associated state post office code standard two letter abbreviation for U.S. states and territories.

STATE_NAME: Associated state name.

COUNTY: Associated county name.

COUNTY_S_S: Associated counties state (Post Office Code) State where the associated county is located; may not be the same as the associated city's state code.

CITY: Associated City name.

FACILITY_N: Official Facility name.

OWNER_TYPE: Airport ownership type.

PU - Publicly owned

PR - Privately owned

MA- Air Force owned

MN - Navy owned

MR - Army owned

FACILITY_USE: Facility Use.

PU - Open to the Public

PR - Private

FAC_OWNER: Facility owner's name.

OWNER_ADDR: Owner's address.

OWNER_CITY: Owner's city of residence.

OWNER_ST_Z: Owner's state of residence and zip code.

OWNER_PHON: Owner's phone number.

FAC_MANAGE: Facility manager's name.

MANAG_ADDR: Manager's address.

MANAG_CITY: Manager's city.

MANAG_ST_Z: Manager's state and zip code.

MANAG_PHON: Manager's phone.

LATITUDE: Airport reference point latitude (Degrees-Minutes-Seconds)

LONGITUDE: Airport reference point longitude (Degrees-Minutes-Seconds)

COORD_DET_: Airport reference point determination method.

E - Estimated

S - Surveyed

ELEVATION: Airport elevation (nearest foot msl). Elevation is measured at the highest point on the centerline of the usable landing surface.

ELEV_DET_M: Airport elevation determination method.

MAGN_VAR: Magnetic variation and determination. Magnetic variation is measured to the nearest degree.

MAGN_VAR_Y: Magnetic variation epoch year.

TRAFFIC_ALT: Traffic pattern altitude. ( Whole feet AGL)

AERO_CHART: Aeronautical sectional chart on which facility appears.

DIST_CBD: Distance from central business district of the associated city to the airport.

DIRECT_CBD: Direction of the airport from the central business district of associated city. (Nearest 1/8 compass point - EX. NE).

ARTCC_IDET: Boundary ARTCC identifier. The boundary ARTCC is the FAA air route traffic control center within whose published boundaries the airport lies. It may not be the controlling ARTCC for the airport if a letter of agreement exists between the boundary ARTCC and another ARTCC.

C_ARTCC_ID: Boundary ARTCC (FAA) computer identifier (EX. ZCW for Washington ARTCC)

ARTCC_NAME: Boundary ARTCC name.

R_ARTCC_ID: Responsible ARTCC identifier. The responsible ARTCC is the FAA air route traffic control center who has assumed control over the airport through a letter of agreement with the boundary ARTCC.

RC_ARTCC_I: Responsible ARTCC (FAA) computer identifier.

RC_ARTCC_N: Responsible ARTCC name.

FSS_LOCAL: Tie-in FSS physically located on facility.

Y - Tie-in FSS is on the airport.

N - tie-in FSS is not on the airport.

FSS_IDENT: Tie-in flight service station (FSS) identifier. (EX. DCA for the Washington FSS.)

FSS_NAME: Tie-in FSS name. (EX. Washington)

NOTAMS_D: Availability of NOTAM 'D' service at airport.

Y - YES

ACTIV_DATE: airport activation date. (MM/YY) Provides the month and year that the facility was added to the NFDC airport database. Note this information is only available for those

facilities opened since 1981.

CERTIFICAT: Airport certification type and date format is a two character code followed by the month/year of certification. Codes AS,BS,CS,DS,ES are for airports having a full certificate under FAR part 139 and receiving scheduling air carrier service from carriers certificated by the Civil Aeronautics Board. The A,B,C,D,E identifies the aircraft rescue and firefighting index for the airport, and the 'S' is for scheduling air carrier service. Codes AU,BU,CU,DU,EU are for airports having a full certificate under FAR part 139 but not currently receiving scheduled service. Code 'LS' is for airports having limited

certification under FAR part 139 and receiving scheduled air carrier service. Code 'LU' is for airports having limited certification under FAR part 139 and not currently receiving

scheduled air carrier service. 'N' or a blank indicates the facility is not certificated.

NASP_FED_C: NASP/FEDERAL agreement code. a combination of 1 to 7 codes that indicate the type of federal agreements existing at the airport. (Ex. NGH)

N- National plan of integrated airport systems.

B- Installation of navigational facilities on privately owned airports under F&E program.

G- Grant agreements under FAAP/ADAP/AIP

H- Compliance with accessibility to the handicapped.

P- Surplus property agreement under public law 289.

R- Surplus property agreement under regulation 16-WAA.

S- Conveyance under section 16, Federal Airport Act of 1946 or

section 23, Airport and Airway development Act of 1970.

V- Advance planning agreement under FAAP.

X- Obligations assumed by transfer

Y- Assurances pursuant to title VI, Civil Rights Act of 1964.

Z- Conveyance under section 303(C), Federal Aviation Act of 1958.

1- Grant agreement has expired; however, agreement remains in effect

for this facility as long as it is public use.

2- Section 303(C) authority from FAA Act of 1958 has expired;

however, agreement remains in effect for this facility as long as it is public use.

3- AP-4 agreement under DLAND or DCLA has expired.

NONE- no grant agreement exists.

BLANK- no grant agreement exists.

EMERG_RIGH: Status of airport that is available for use during a national emergency. These are civil airports that were formerly military airfields but the military services have a continuing

interest in their use during national emergencies. Data values are one or a series of alphanumeric codes indicating national emergency status.

1- Airports certificated under FAR part 139.

2- Civil Airport where military use is subject to lease.

3P-Airport is partially released from National Emergency use provision.

3E- Airport is entirely released from National Emergenct use provision.

4- Airport includes surplus real property which has been conveyed for, or converted to, revenue production.

5- Exclusive military use airport.

6- The airport is in the process of disposal or reversion.

7P- A letter of intent has been issued to release a part of the airport property.

7E- A letter of intent has been issued to release the entire airport property.

8- An exclusive right has been granted (whether or not in violation of an agreement.)

8P- An exclusive right has been granted (whether or not in violation of an agreement); however, this exclusive right is of the proprietary type.

8N- An exclusive right exists through a P.L. 80-289 deed providing exemption for fuel and oil sales; however, an exclusive right for fuel oil sales has not been granted.

ATTEN_SCHE: Airport attendence schedule (When minimum services are available) Format is months/days/hours where the first part describes the months that the facility is attended, then the days of the week that the facility is open, and finally the hours

within the day that it is attended. This field may also contain 'UNATNDD' for unattended facilities. If there are different schedules for different times of the year the schedules are

separated by a space (JAN-MAY/ALL/ALL JUNE-DEC/ALL/SR-SS).

LIGHT_SCHE: Airport lighting schedule. Value is the beginning-ending times (local time) That lights are operated, Format can be 1900-2300, DUSK-0100, ALL, DUSK-DAWN, NONE,ETC.
